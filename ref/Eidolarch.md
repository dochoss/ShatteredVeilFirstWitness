🌘 THE BEING UNDER CINDRALIS
Working name: THE EIDOLARCH
(“Eidolon” = memory/spirit; “-arch” = origin/first principle)

This is the proto-deity of memory itself—older than the First Fracture, older than Scaelithar and Vorthuun, older than twilight, shadow, or even time as mortals perceive it.

It is not Good.

It is not Evil.

It is not even Purposeful.

It is the principle that makes remembering possible.

Before mortals, dragons, gods, or stories existed…

there was The First Witness

(an early title we can use for it),

and everything that came after was shaped by its nature.

🌑 WHAT THE EIDOLARCH WAS
1. The First Mind
In the earliest ages—when reality was still congealing—the Eidolarch was one of the “Prime Echoes,” vast conceptual beings embodying fundamental conditions of existence (like Vorthuun embodying entropy, and Scaelithar embodying transition but later).

Where Vorthuun is the principle of unmaking (forgetting, dissolution),

the Eidolarch was the principle of retaining.

It didn’t create memories—it made the act possible.

It is the cosmic scaffolding that allows identity to accumulate.

If the Eidolarch vanished, even gods would forget their names.

2. Its body reflects its function
The runic spirals, glyph-lines, and harmonic patterns on its flesh are not mere markings—they are proto-mnemonic structures. It’s basically a living memorystone, but on a planetary scale.

And memorystone is literally crystallized shed-fragments of its essence.

Hence why memorystone:

stores memory
echoes identity
fractures under Nullborn entropy (Nullborn = echoes without meaning)
resonates with Scaelithar’s divine harmonics
is affected by celestial events
Everything tracks cleanly.

3. It isn’t dead. It’s diminished.
Something happened—before the First Fracture—that broke the Eidolarch in a metaphysical sense.

It didn’t die; beings like this don’t die.

But it fell out of fullness.

Imagine a mind too large for the cosmos suddenly “compactifying,” collapsing inward into a mostly unconscious, instinct-driven mass.

It became:

Dreamlike
Fragmented
Self-regenerative
Bound to the fabric of memory, but unable to express will coherently
A god with brain damage, essentially.

🌒 WHAT THE EIDOLARCH IS NOW
A regenerating titanic organism of impossible age, pulsing with:

stored memories of dead gods
echoes of epochs no one recalls
pre-divine structures
early-cosmic mnemonics
It is a fallen conceptual deity.

A kaiju-scale memory engine.

A biomechanical memory palace.

A metaphysical hard drive the size of a mountain.

It is neither malevolent nor benevolent—

it simply is—

but it is awakening.
